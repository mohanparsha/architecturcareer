The Convergence Advantage

The highest-value cybersecurity professionals are those who cross domain boundaries — an Offensive Security specialist who understands Cloud, an AppSec engineer who can read GRC risk reports, a SOC analyst who understands IAM. Plan your foundation first, specialise deeply in one domain, then deliberately acquire adjacent domain knowledge after 3 years. This combination commands the highest salaries and the most interesting work.

🧭 Quick Self-Assessment

Select one option below that best describes your natural inclination. Don't overthink — trust your instinct.

⚔️

"I love finding weak spots. I want to think like the attacker — legally."

You're drawn to breaking things to make them stronger. Puzzles, CTFs, and the thrill of exploitation excite you.

🔍

"I prefer defending. When an attack hits, I want to detect it and shut it down."

You want to be on the front line of cyber defence — monitoring, analyzing, and responding in real time.

💻

"I care about writing secure code. Security should be built in from day one."

You want to embed security into the development process, catch vulnerabilities early, and build resilient software.

☁️

"Cloud infrastructure excites me — AWS, Kubernetes, containers."

You're drawn to the scale and complexity of cloud environments and want to secure them.

📋

"Policies, risk, and compliance make sense to me. I like the big-picture 'why'."

You think in terms of governance, regulation, and organisational risk. You bridge security and business.

🔑

"Access control — who should have what permission — is fascinating to me."

You care about identity, zero trust, and making sure the right people access the right resources.

🌐

"Protocols, traffic, firewalls. I want to own the network perimeter."

Packets, routing, and network architecture speak to you. You want to protect the communication layer.

🎯

"I'm unsure — everything sounds interesting."

That's perfectly fine. Start broad with Security+, build your foundation, and your preference will emerge.

18-Month Roadmap

Click any phase to expand its action items. Every milestone is achievable with free resources.

Months 1-3

Foundation — Build Your Base

Networking, Linux, scripting fundamentals, and your first free certification.

  • Complete TryHackMe Pre-Security learning path
  • Register & pass (ISC)² CC — completely free certification
  • Join the null community India chapter in your city
  • Master Linux CLI basics via OverTheWire Bandit
  • Start Python scripting with Automate the Boring Stuff
Months 4-6

Domain Selection — Choose Your Path

Pick your domain from the self-assessment. Begin domain-specific labs and certification prep.

  • Use the "Find Your Fit" quiz to confirm your domain
  • Start domain-specific labs on TryHackMe / HackTheBox
  • Identify your entry-level certification target
  • Follow 10+ domain practitioners on LinkedIn & X
  • Watch domain-specific conference talks (YouTube is free)
Months 7-9

First Certification — Prove Your Skills

Sit CompTIA Security+ or equivalent. Attend your first community meetup. Join a CTF team.

  • Prepare for and sit CompTIA Security+ (or domain cert)
  • Attend first null community / OWASP chapter meetup
  • Join a CTF team — PicoCTF, TryHackMe competitions
  • Start documenting your learning journey publicly
  • Ask one practitioner 3-5 years ahead for 15 min of advice
Months 10-12

Portfolio Building — Show Your Work

GitHub lab writeups, technical blog, and open-source contributions build your professional credibility.

  • Document 5+ lab writeups on GitHub with clear methodology
  • Start a technical blog (Medium, Hashnode, or personal site)
  • Contribute to an open-source security tool on GitHub
  • Build 1-2 projects showcasing your domain skills
  • Get your first peer review from a community member
Months 13-15

Internship & Bug Bounty — Real World Exposure

Apply for security internships, register on bug bounty platforms, attend Nullcon or regional events.

  • Apply for security internships (Big 4, product cos, consultancies)
  • Register on HackerOne / Bugcrowd — start beginner programs
  • Attend Nullcon, C0c0n, or a regional security conference
  • Get your first real-world finding or internship experience
  • Build relationships with 3+ practitioners in your domain
Months 16-18

Job-Ready — Launch Your Career

Second certification, polished portfolio, optimised LinkedIn, active network, and targeted applications.

  • Earn second domain-specific certification
  • Have 2-3 strong portfolio projects ready for interviews
  • Optimise LinkedIn — headline, summary, skills, endorsements
  • Actively network at community events and online
  • Apply to target companies — SOC, AppSec, GRC, Pentest roles

🎯 The Golden Rule

Domain selection is not permanent. Many of the best professionals have crossed domains multiple times. What matters is starting. Pick the one that excites you most, go 12 months deep, then reassess with real experience under your belt. You discover your domain by doing, not by thinking.
